### Figure 2. Component Necessity (Leave-One-Out)
**File**: `docs/figure/Fig2_ComponentNecessity.png`  
**Script**: `src/visualization/plot_figure2_component.py`  
**Data**: ds000243, N=52, Schaefer 200 (cortical), TR=2.5s, short=48 TRs (120s), 10 seeds, 50 bootstraps, Fisher z correction

**Panel A. Extrapolated Reliability by Condition.**
Range bars (IQR: Q1–Q3) with subsampled scatter dots and mean±SD diamond for each of six conditions: Full Pipeline (ρ̂T = 0.838 ± 0.030), w/o Spearman-Brown (0.675 ± 0.057), w/o Ledoit-Wolf (0.832 ± 0.030), w/o Bootstrap (0.888 ± 0.026), w/o Bayesian Prior (0.753 ± 0.043), and w/o Attenuation Correction (0.925 ± 0.023). Dashed blue line indicates the full pipeline mean. Each dot represents one subject × seed combination (N = 520 per condition; subsampled to 350 for clarity).

**Panel B. Performance Drop When Removed.**
Bar chart of −Δρ̂T (positive = performance drop upon removal = essential component; negative = removal increases ρ̂T = component prevents overcorrection). Error bars show ±1 SD (one-sided: upward for positive bars, downward for negative bars). Significance markers (***) indicate Bonferroni-corrected paired t-tests comparing each ablated condition to Full Pipeline (subject-level means across 10 seeds, N = 52).

**Statistical testing.** A Friedman test (non-parametric repeated-measures ANOVA) across six conditions confirmed a significant overall effect: χ²(5) = 258.90, p = 6.76 × 10⁻⁵⁴. Post-hoc paired t-tests (two-tailed, Bonferroni-corrected for 5 comparisons) were conducted on subject-level means (averaged across 10 seeds per subject, N = 52) comparing each ablated condition to the Full Pipeline:

| Component Removed | Δρ̂T | t(51) | p_corrected | Cohen's d |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| w/o Spearman-Brown | −0.163 | 37.47 | 4.4 × 10⁻³⁸ | 5.25 |
| w/o Bayesian Prior | −0.085 | 44.29 | 1.1 × 10⁻⁴¹ | 6.20 |
| w/o Ledoit-Wolf | −0.006 | 14.44 | 6.0 × 10⁻¹⁹ | 2.02 |
| w/o Bootstrap | +0.050 | −40.22 | 1.3 × 10⁻³⁹ | −5.63 |
| w/o Attenuation Corr. | +0.087 | −36.10 | 2.7 × 10⁻³⁷ | −5.06 |

All comparisons reached p < 0.001 (***). Kendall's W = 0.996.

**Interpretation.** Spearman-Brown prophecy (Δ = −0.163) and Bayesian empirical prior (Δ = −0.085) are the two critical components — their removal causes the largest performance drops. Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage has a statistically significant but practically negligible effect (Δ = −0.006). Removing bootstrap resampling or attenuation correction inflates ρ̂T above the full pipeline value, indicating these components serve as regularizers that prevent overcorrection.
