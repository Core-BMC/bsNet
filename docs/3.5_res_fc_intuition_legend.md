## Figure Legend

**Figure X. Intuitive visualization of BS-NET's reliability correction at the FC matrix level.**

BS-NET estimates a scalar reliability coefficient (ρ̂T) rather than reconstructing per-connection FC values. To bridge the gap between scalar improvement and the FC matrices familiar to resting-state fMRI researchers, we visualize the FC matrix at BS-NET's empirically determined equivalent scan duration alongside the direct paired-subject correction results.

**(A–C) Individual-level FC matrices (representative subject, sub-013).**
FC matrices (Schaefer 200 parcels, 7 Yeo networks) computed from (A) 2 minutes (48 TRs) of resting-state data without shrinkage regularization (Raw FC), (B) 4 minutes (95 TRs) corresponding to BS-NET's equivalent scan duration, and (C) the remaining 15 minutes (368 TRs) with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage as reference. Rows and columns are sorted by Yeo 7-network assignment; black lines denote network boundaries. The representative subject (sub-013) was selected by minimizing deviation from the group mean r_FC while maximizing structural similarity to the group-average FC pattern (sim_ref = 0.805). The progression from A to C illustrates how short-scan noise—particularly in between-network regions—is progressively reduced with longer acquisitions.

**(D–F) Group-average FC matrices (N = 52).**
Same three conditions as Panels A–C, averaged across all 52 subjects. Group averaging attenuates individual-level noise, revealing consistent network structure across conditions. The between-network noise visible in Panel D (2 min) is reduced in Panel E (4 min) and largely absent in Panel F (reference), confirming that the individual-level pattern in Row 1 is systematic rather than idiosyncratic.

**(G) Edge-level attenuation (sub-013, 19,900 unique connections).**
Scatter plot of all unique ROI-pair FC values at 2 minutes (amber) and 4 minutes (blue) versus the reference FC. Regression lines quantify the attenuation effect: the 2-minute FC has a slope of 0.49 (r_FC = 0.635), indicating that short-scan FC captures only ~49% of true FC variation. At 4 minutes, slope increases to 0.57 (r_FC = 0.705). Points are subsampled (N = 5,000 per condition) for visual clarity. The dashed diagonal represents perfect agreement. The gap between the regression line and the diagonal reflects the systematic attenuation that BS-NET's reliability correction addresses.

**(H) Equivalent scan duration.**
Duration–performance curves from the ds000243 duration sweep (N = 49 subjects, 10 seeds per duration). The amber curve shows observed r_FC between short-scan FC and reference FC as a function of scan duration; the blue curve shows BS-NET's corrected ρ̂T. Shaded bands indicate ±1 SD across subjects. At 2 minutes, BS-NET corrects r_FC = 0.714 ± 0.054 to ρ̂T = 0.825 ± 0.029 (red vertical arrow, Δ = +0.111). This ρ̂T corresponds to an observed r_FC at approximately 240 seconds (4 minutes) on the duration curve (blue horizontal arrow), establishing the "equivalent scan duration" interpretation: **BS-NET makes a 2-minute scan perform like a 4-minute scan in terms of FC reliability.** Beyond ~350 seconds, ρ̂T falls below r_FC (dashed segment), indicating that correction is no longer beneficial at longer durations. Three subjects (sub-004, sub-006, sub-008) were excluded from the sweep due to total scan durations below 600 seconds, which provided insufficient reference data for the 450-second sweep condition.

**(I) BS-NET correction at the individual level (N = 49).**
Paired comparison of raw r_FC (2 min) versus BS-NET's corrected ρ̂T for each subject. Green lines connect each subject's pre- and post-correction values. Red diamonds with error bars indicate group mean ± SD. BS-NET improved similarity to reference FC in all 49 subjects (100%), with a mean improvement of Δ = +0.111 ± 0.026. Notably, BS-NET also reduced inter-subject variability (SD from 0.054 to 0.029), suggesting that the correction stabilizes estimates across individuals. This panel provides direct evidence of BS-NET's actual output, complementing the indirect FC matrix visualization in Panels B and E.

**Panel B/E rationale.** BS-NET estimates scalar reliability (ρ̂T) rather than per-connection corrected FC. Panels B and E display FC matrices at the equivalent scan duration (4 min ≈ 240 s) as an empirical proxy for the quality level that BS-NET achieves. The equivalence is established through the duration–performance curve in Panel H, where ρ̂T at 2 minutes (0.825) corresponds to observed r_FC at approximately 4 minutes (0.826). Panel I provides the direct, non-proxy evidence of BS-NET's correction magnitude.

## Design Decisions

1. **Why 4-minute FC as proxy?** BS-NET outputs scalar ρ̂T, not per-connection FC. The 4-minute FC matrix is shown because ρ̂T(2 min) = 0.825 ≈ r_FC(240 s) = 0.826 on the empirical duration curve. This allows FC-matrix-level visualization of what the scalar improvement "looks like."

2. **Why non-overlapping reference?** Reference FC is computed from ts[95:] (368 TRs) rather than the full timeseries to avoid positive bias from shared timepoints. This is conservative: the reference is slightly noisier than a full-scan reference but methodologically cleaner.

3. **Why sub-013 as representative?** Selected by composite scoring: (a) r_FC close to group mean, (b) Δr_FC close to group mean, (c) highest structural similarity (sim_ref = 0.805) between individual reference FC and group-average reference FC. This ensures the individual panels show a pattern consistent with the group-level results.

4. **Why dashed line after ~350 s in Panel H?** At long durations, ρ̂T drops below r_FC because BS-NET's Spearman-Brown extrapolation overshoots when the short scan already captures most of the signal. The dashed rendering signals that BS-NET correction is designed for, and beneficial at, short scan durations.

5. **N = 49 vs N = 52.** Three subjects (sub-004, sub-006, sub-008) have only ~598 s of total scan time (239 TRs instead of 463 TRs). The duration sweep requires scan durations up to 450 s, leaving insufficient reference data for these subjects. They are included in the FC matrix panels (A–F, N = 52) but excluded from the duration sweep and Panel I (N = 49).
