### Figure 7: FC-Based ADHD vs Control Classification — Multi-Seed BS-NET Enhancement
**File**: `Figure7_ADHD_Classification.png`

**Experimental design**:
- **Subjects**: N=40 (20 ADHD, 20 Control) from ADHD-200 Preprocessed
- **Atlases**: CC200 (195 ROIs, 18,915 edges) and CC400 (356 ROIs, 63,190 edges)
- **Classifier**: Linear SVM (C=1.0, StandardScaler preprocessing)
- **Cross-validation**: Stratified 5-fold × 5 repeats per seed
- **Bootstrap seeds**: 10 independent seeds for BS-NET FC generation (seed stability test)
- **Three FC conditions compared**:
  1. **Raw FC (2 min)**: Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage FC from short scan (~120s equivalent)
  2. **BS-NET (2 min)**: Edge-level corrected FC via bootstrap extrapolation (Fisher z method)
  3. **Reference FC (full scan)**: Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage FC from full available scan (154–352s)
- **Features**: Upper triangle of ROI×ROI FC matrix (vectorized)
- **Aggregation**: Per-seed accuracy/AUC averaged across seeds; fold-level std computed from all folds across all seeds

**(A) Classification Accuracy (10 seeds)**: Grouped bar chart comparing accuracy across three FC conditions for CC200 (blue) and CC400 (amber). Bar height = mean accuracy across 10 bootstrap seeds; error bars = ±1 SD across all CV folds (10 seeds × 25 folds = 250 observations). Scattered points show individual fold accuracies (jittered, α=0.40). Gray dashed line marks chance level (0.50).

**(B) Area Under ROC Curve (10 seeds)**: Same layout for AUC. Scattered points show per-fold AUC values.

**(C) Accuracy Change vs Raw FC**: Δ Accuracy relative to raw FC baseline. Scattered points show per-seed deltas (10 per atlas). BS-NET shows consistent positive delta across seeds; Reference FC shows stable negative delta.

**(D) Summary**: Tabulated results with key experimental parameters. For multi-seed runs, σs (seed-level standard deviation of accuracy) is reported to quantify bootstrap stability.

## Results Interpretation

### Key Findings

1. **BS-NET ≥ Raw FC across 10 seeds**: BS-NET corrected FC consistently matches or exceeds raw FC classification accuracy in both atlases. The direction is stable across 10 independent bootstrap seeds with low seed-level variability (σs < 0.01), confirming that the BS-NET advantage is not a single-seed artifact.

2. **Reference FC paradox**: The full-scan reference FC yields the lowest classification accuracy. This is explained by the short total scan duration in ADHD-200 (77–176 volumes, 154–352s):
   - The "full" scan is only 2.5–6× longer than the "short" scan, providing minimal additional signal
   - Raw short-scan FC benefits from Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage regularization, which reduces noise and overfitting in high-dimensional feature space
   - The reference FC, while more precise for global similarity (rFC), may contain more noise at the edge level when used as SVM features

3. **Multi-seed stability**: Seed-level standard deviation (σs) of accuracy < 0.01 for all conditions and atlases. This demonstrates that classification performance is robust to the specific random seed used in BS-NET bootstrap, consistent with the multi-seed ρ̂T stability observed in Track G/ADHD validation (seed std < 0.005).

4. **Comparison to prior literature**:
   - ADHD-200 Global Competition best imaging accuracy: 60.5% (3-class, Brown et al. 2012)
   - Graph distance + SVM: 73.6% (dos Santos Siqueira et al. 2014)
   - CC200 Deep Learning AUC: 0.69 (PMC 2020)
   - Our results are within the expected range for N=40

### Limitations

- **Small sample size**: N=40 with 5-fold CV → 8 subjects per fold, leading to high variance (SD ≈ 0.15)
- **Feature selection**: No dimensionality reduction applied; PCA, network-specific features, or graph metrics may improve performance
- **Short reference scan**: ADHD-200 total scan durations (154–352s) are short by modern standards; this limits the "upper bound" interpretation of the reference condition
- **Single classifier**: Only linear SVM tested; non-linear methods may capture more complex FC patterns

### Clinical Implication

BS-NET corrected FC from a 2-minute scan preserves — and does not degrade — clinically relevant group differences for ADHD classification. Multi-seed testing confirms this result is reproducible across different bootstrap initializations. This supports BS-NET's practical utility: a 2-minute rsfMRI scan processed through BS-NET can serve as input to downstream diagnostic classification without information loss relative to the raw short-scan FC.
