### Figure 5: Multi-Seed BS-NET Validation on ABIDE PCP — CC200 (N=468, 10 seeds)
**File**: `Figure5_ABIDE_MultiSeed_CC200.png`
**Script**: `src/scripts/plot_abide_results.py --multi-seed 10 --correction-method fisher_z`

**(a) Raw FC vs BS-NET (multi-seed)**: Scatter plot of r_FC (2-min raw correlation) versus mean ρ̂T across 10 seeds with 95% seed confidence intervals. The systematic upward shift from the identity line demonstrates consistent BS-NET improvement. Fisher z-space correction eliminates ceiling saturation at ρ̂T = 1.0.
**(b) Seed Variability**: Horizontal box plots showing per-subject ρ̂T distributions across 10 bootstrap seeds, overlaid with r_FC raw markers (×). Mean seed standard deviation = 0.005, indicating high reproducibility of the extrapolation.
**(c) Improvement Distribution**: Histogram of Δ = ρ̂T_mean − r_FC. The distribution is right-shifted with mean Δ = +0.072 (N=468), confirming population-level improvement. 0% ceiling effect under Fisher z correction.
**(d) Prediction Stability**: Per-subject seed standard deviation σ(ρ̂T) sorted ascending, demonstrating uniformly low variability (< 0.02 for all subjects).

**Key statistics**: ρ̂T = 0.843 ± 0.036, seed std = 0.005, ceiling (>0.999) = 0/468 (0.0%).

### Figure 7: Ceiling Effect Analysis — CC200 (N=468, 4-Method Comparison)
**File**: `Figure7_ABIDE_Ceiling_CC200.png`
**Script**: `src/scripts/analyze_ceiling_effect.py --atlas cc200 --n-jobs 8`

4-panel comparison of attenuation correction methods demonstrating the ceiling effect problem and its resolution:

**(a) Original CTT correction**: Standard Spearman-Brown + hard clip to [−1, 1]. 53.6% of subjects saturate at ρ̂T ≥ 0.999 (ceiling effect), producing an artificial spike at the upper bound. Mean ρ̂T = 0.967 ± 0.058.
**(b) Fisher z-space correction**: Additive correction in arctanh(z) space with tanh back-transform. 0% ceiling effect, principled bounding, theoretically grounded (Shou 2014, Teeuw 2021). Mean ρ̂T = 0.843 ± 0.036.
**(c) Partial correction (α=0.5)**: Damped correction using Zimmerman (2007) partial attenuation. 0% ceiling, intermediate improvement. Mean ρ̂T = 0.885.
**(d) Soft clamp (tanh compression)**: Rank-preserving tanh compression. 0% ceiling but over-dampened. Mean ρ̂T = 0.798.

**Conclusion**: Fisher z-space correction is adopted as the default method — it completely eliminates the ceiling effect while preserving meaningful improvement magnitude and maintaining theoretical defensibility.
